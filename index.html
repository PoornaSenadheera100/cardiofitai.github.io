Autonomous Detection of Cardiac Abnormalities with Customized Dietary Planning

Learn More

Project Scope

This project aims to address the growing global challenge of cardiovascular disease by offering a more accessible solution for cardiac diagnostics and personalized care. Our innovative method generates a full 12-lead ECG using only a single Lead I ECG, powered by a modified version of the advanced Attention U-Net deep learning framework. This approach enables more comprehensive cardiac evaluations, facilitating the detection of conditions such as ischemic heart disease, conduction abnormalities, and heart attacks. In addition, we are extending this research to offer personalized dietary recommendations based on individual factors like age, gender, BMI, diabetes, cholesterol levels, and heart health. By combining ECG reconstruction with tailored diagnostic and dietary guidance, our project offers a holistic approach to improving cardiovascular health management.

Literature Survey

The acquisition of ECG signals from the palm has gained attention due to its non-intrusive and user-friendly nature. Lourenço et al. [1] developed a palm-based ECG device using dry Ag/AgCl electrodes and a custom signal conditioning circuit, simplifying traditional three-lead setups into a two-lead configuration without the need for conductive gels. Adil et al. [2] further contributed by proposing a real-time two-electrode ECG system that uses the AD8232 analog front end and an Atmega328p microcontroller, effectively reducing noise through a combination of hardware and digital filtering techniques, enabling robust palm-based ECG acquisition for health monitoring systems.

Reconstructing multiple ECG leads from fewer inputs has been a key research area, particularly in applications aiming to reduce the number of required electrodes. Garg et al. [3] introduced a modified Attention U-Net framework for generating a full 12-lead ECG from a single lead, achieving high correlation and accuracy in lead reconstruction, which shows great potential for disease classification. Additionally, advancements in heart disease diagnosis using 12-lead ECGs and deep learning have shown promise, as demonstrated by Zhang et al. [4], who utilized a deep neural network to classify multiple cardiac conditions with improved diagnostic accuracy by extracting essential temporal and statistical features. Given the rising demand for personalized healthcare, integrating dietary recommendations based on individual patient profiles, including age, BMI, and cholesterol levels, provides a comprehensive approach to managing cardiovascular health.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

12-Lead ECG Acquisition Using Convenient Method

Traditional ECG systems require multiple electrodes across the body. Our research focuses on a more accessible method using palm-based electrodes to acquire a reliable 12-Lead ECG, simplifying the process while maintaining clinical accuracy.

Disease Diagnosis


Conventional diagnosis relies on expert analysis of 12-lead ECGs. Our study uses deep learning models to automate this process, allowing for faster and more accurate detection of cardiac conditions, even in non-clinical settings.

Personalized Dietary Advice


Most dietary plans are generic and lack personalization. Our approach provides tailored dietary advice by considering key health metrics such as age, BMI, cholesterol levels, and cardiac status, improving the effectiveness of dietary interventions.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

"How to obtain a non-invasive, cost-effective 12-lead ECG for early detection of heart conditions and provide personalized dietary advice in real time?"

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death worldwide, causing 17.9 million deaths annually. Current 12-lead ECG acquisition methods are costly, complex, and impractical for regular use in remote areas, limiting early diagnosis and continuous monitoring of cardiac abnormalities. Existing methods for providing personalized dietary recommendations often overlook key physiological factors like cholesterol, glucose levels, and heart health, resulting in suboptimal health outcomes.

Proposed Solution

This research presents a palm-based ECG acquisition system that simplifies 12-lead ECG generation using a three-electrode setup. The system captures a Lead I ECG, which is processed through a deep learning model to reconstruct the remaining leads, offering a cost-effective, non-invasive, and accessible tool for early detection of cardiac conditions. Additionally, it integrates personalized dietary advice by analyzing factors such as age, BMI, and heart condition, allowing individuals to manage their heart health proactively. The system offers real-time monitoring, making it highly useful in remote or resource-limited areas.

Research Objectives

  • Develop the palm-based ECG acquisition system that captures a Lead I ECG.
  • Reconstruct the remaining 11 leads using a deep learning model.
  • Perform the diagnosis based on the reconstructed 12-lead ECG.
  • Integrate personalized dietary advice based on the relevant factors.
  • Validate the system's accuracy and effectiveness in early detection of cardiac conditions.

Methodology

The proposed system consists of four main components:

  1. Acquisition of ECG signal from palm-based approach.
  2. Reconstructing remaining 11 ECG leads with high level of accuracy from lead I ECG signal.
  3. Identification of critical cardiac diseases, allowing for informed discussion.
  4. Proposing dietary advice based on patient health records.

This system diagram represents a healthcare platform designed to capture and analyze user health data, such as ECG signals and medical reports, through a smart tablet interface. Users can scan their palms to acquire ECG readings. The collected data is sent to Firebase, which serves as the central database, where it is processed by deep learning models hosted on a Flask server. The system includes two key AI models: CardioFit’s Modified Attention U-net for reconstructing multi-lead ECG signals from single-lead data and a CNN Baseline Model for diagnosing cardiac diseases.

In addition to medical data analysis, the platform integrates with AWS Amazon Textract to extract relevant information from scanned medical reports. This information is combined with ECG data, BMI, and dietary advice to provide comprehensive health insights to users. The system generates notifications to alert loved ones or paramedics in case of emergencies, while also offering personalized medicine reminders powered by GPT-4. Through this integrated approach, the system ensures timely interventions, accurate diagnosis, and user-friendly health management.
