Autonomous Detection of Cardiac Abnormalities with Customized Dietary Planning

Learn More

Project Scope

This project aims to address the growing global challenge of cardiovascular disease by offering a more accessible solution for cardiac diagnostics and personalized care. Our innovative method generates a full 12-lead ECG using only a single Lead I ECG, powered by a modified version of the advanced Attention U-Net deep learning framework. This approach enables more comprehensive cardiac evaluations, facilitating the detection of conditions such as ischemic heart disease, conduction abnormalities, and heart attacks. In addition, we are extending this research to offer personalized dietary recommendations based on individual factors like age, gender, BMI, diabetes, cholesterol levels, and heart health. By combining ECG reconstruction with tailored diagnostic and dietary guidance, our project offers a holistic approach to improving cardiovascular health management.

Literature Survey

The acquisition of ECG signals from the palm has gained attention due to its non-intrusive and user-friendly nature. Lourenço et al. [1] developed a palm-based ECG device using dry Ag/AgCl electrodes and a custom signal conditioning circuit, simplifying traditional three-lead setups into a two-lead configuration without the need for conductive gels. Adil et al. [2] further contributed by proposing a real-time two-electrode ECG system that uses the AD8232 analog front end and an Atmega328p microcontroller, effectively reducing noise through a combination of hardware and digital filtering techniques, enabling robust palm-based ECG acquisition for health monitoring systems.

Reconstructing multiple ECG leads from fewer inputs has been a key research area, particularly in applications aiming to reduce the number of required electrodes. Garg et al. [3] introduced a modified Attention U-Net framework for generating a full 12-lead ECG from a single lead, achieving high correlation and accuracy in lead reconstruction, which shows great potential for disease classification. Additionally, advancements in heart disease diagnosis using 12-lead ECGs and deep learning have shown promise, as demonstrated by Zhang et al. [4], who utilized a deep neural network to classify multiple cardiac conditions with improved diagnostic accuracy by extracting essential temporal and statistical features. Given the rising demand for personalized healthcare, integrating dietary recommendations based on individual patient profiles, including age, BMI, and cholesterol levels, provides a comprehensive approach to managing cardiovascular health.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

12-Lead ECG Acquisition Using Convenient Method

Traditional ECG systems require multiple electrodes across the body. Our research focuses on a more accessible method using palm-based electrodes to acquire a reliable Lead I ECG, simplifying the process while maintaining clinical accuracy.

Disease Diagnosis


Conventional diagnosis relies on expert analysis of 12-lead ECGs. Our study uses deep learning models to automate this process, allowing for faster and more accurate detection of cardiac conditions, even in non-clinical settings.

Personalized Dietary Plan


Most dietary plans are generic and lack personalization. Our approach provides tailored dietary advice by considering key health metrics such as age, BMI, cholesterol levels, and cardiac status, improving the effectiveness of dietary interventions.

Methodology

The primary ECG lead, Lead I, is obtained from the palms using disposable electrodes. A modified attention U-Net deep learning model is used to reconstruct the remaining 11 ECG leads. The entire 12-lead ECG is then used as input for a 1D convolutional neural network (CNN) model to diagnose 20 different cardiac diseases. This approach allows for rapid, accurate, and cost-effective cardiac screening without hospital visits.

Using the reconstructed 12-lead ECG, the model achieved high accuracy in diagnosing multiple cardiac conditions. The deep learning model provided a viable solution for early cardiac diagnosis, thereby helping to identify patients who need immediate medical attention.
